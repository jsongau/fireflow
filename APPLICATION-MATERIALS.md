## 2. Formal cover letter (use if the application requires one)

Dear `[HIRING MANAGER]`,

I am writing to apply for the Manager, Customer Experience position at Samyang America. Customer experience for a consumer products company is not only answering messages. It is knowing the product, understanding the order, protecting the customer relationship, and making sure Sales, Supply Chain, Finance, Quality, and Operations are working from the same truth. To show how I approach that, I built a working model of it.

FireFlow (https://samyang-xi.vercel.app) is an independent customer experience operating model organized around Samyang's public U.S. portfolio. It carries a product from a first question through a routed, owned, resolved case, keeps the product, evidence, owner, and next customer update attached the whole way, and turns repeated issues into a continuous-improvement loop. Its order-to-cash chapter walks one synthetic retail order through order entry, pricing, delivery, billing, and a retailer deduction, with clear ownership at each step.

My experience is more than eight years across consumer products, retail customer operations, billing, customer communication, process training, and complex issue resolution. That is where I learned that accurate information, clear ownership, and calm communication decide whether a process works.

I want to be direct about scope. FireFlow demonstrates that I understand the SAP SD and order-to-cash workflow at a process level. It is not an SAP implementation and does not claim access to Samyang's systems. My strength is the operational layer around the system: what the customer needs, what information must be accurate, what is blocking progress, who owns the next action, and how the update is communicated. I am ready to learn and support Samyang's actual environment.

I would welcome the chance to discuss the role.
