# Cover letter source material

This was the `EmployerIntro` cover section, removed from the site on 2026-07-09 because it
loaded above every route and made navigation between pages feel heavy. The argument in it
belongs in a cover letter, not in the product chrome. Kept here verbatim first, then broken
down into what is usable and what needs checking.

---

## What to keep

**The framing sentence is the strongest thing here.** "An independent customer experience
operating model, built to show how I think." It says what the artifact is and why it exists
in eleven words, and it does not oversell. It should open the cover letter.

**The honesty clause is a feature, not fine print.** Most candidates would bury "synthetic
and labeled." Leading with it says something about how you would handle a customer's data,
and a hiring manager who has ever been burned by a confident wrong number will notice.

**The list of what it covers** (product knowledge, order flow, customer communication,
escalation, billing friction, issue resolution, continuous improvement) maps almost
one-to-one onto the posting's responsibilities. Keep the mapping; drop the list format,
which reads as a résumé restated.

---

## What needs checking before it goes in a cover letter

**"More than eight years."** This is the one claim in the paragraph that a recruiter will
test against the résumé, and it must match it exactly. Nowhere else on the site does a
tenure number appear, deliberately. Before this sentence goes anywhere, confirm the number
against the actual dates on the résumé, and confirm what the years are *of*. "Eight years
across consumer products, retail customer operations, billing, customer communication,
process training, and complex issue resolution" is six domains in one clause, which reads
as padding even when every word is true. Pick the two that matter to this posting.

**"Operating model" is doing a lot of work.** Be ready for "what do you mean by operating
model?" The answer that holds up: an intake taxonomy with 29 procedures, an escalation
standard that executes rather than sits in a binder, a case lifecycle with a named owner and
a date at every stage, and a pattern trigger that turns repeated cases into a root cause. All
four are on the site and can be opened.

**Never say "the intake console is the operating system."** It reads as a slogan, and the
site's own writing rules ban the construction. What you mean is that customer experience is
the front layer of the company's operating system, and that is worth saying plainly and once.

---

## The claim that must not appear

No SAP implementation, configuration, integration, or tenure claim. The defensible version,
which is already the site's own language:

> I have not configured SAP. I have read SAP SD and I own the process around it, and nearly
> every dispute I have worked traced back to a field on a record like one of these.

And the posted band is **$101,000 to $122,000**. Whatever the letter says, the number in your
head should match that range before the recruiter screen, not after.

## Where the site says this better than the letter can

The letter should point at, not describe:

- `/` the homepage teaser: one short shipment, and the lifecycle that works it before the ad runs.
- `/ops` the case board, with the featured case traced end to end.
- `/leadership#sop-register` the 29 procedures, each with an owner, a metric, and a resolve target.
- `/leadership#team` the approval ladder, whose numbers are the same numbers the intake check runs on.
- `/about#why` the honest account of what is invented and why.

One line each. The site is the argument; the letter is the invitation to read it.
