# 00 — Executive Summary

**Prepared:** 2026-07-07 · **Status:** Core doc, complete

## What the homepage must accomplish

The FireFlow Product Intelligence homepage is the front door of an independent portfolio project. It has one job: make a hiring manager at Samyang America believe, from the experience itself, that Nathan J. Song can run a Customer Experience function for a fast-moving CPG food brand. Not tell them — show them. The page has to demonstrate product knowledge, customer-and-vendor operations judgment, escalation and resolution discipline, transparent measurement, cross-functional thinking, and enough technical craft (interaction, accessibility, state) to signal that he can partner credibly with technology, ERP, EDI, and data teams.

## Who it is for

Three audiences, in priority order:

1. **The recruiter / hiring manager** (primary). Skims first, needs the concept in ~90 seconds, then rewards depth. The hero, the chapter nav, and one or two signature interactions must land immediately; the resolution simulator and methodology reward the engaged reader.
2. **The simulated consumer** (a persona the page performs). A person with a missing sauce packet, an allergen question, a crushed bag of chips. Their journey shows Nathan's consumer-care instincts.
3. **The simulated vendor / retailer / distributor** (a persona the page performs). A buyer with a partial fill, a deduction, an EDI failure, a sell-sheet request. Their journey shows the retailer-and-distributor experience the job explicitly centers on.

## How it supports the Samyang application

The target role's own words define the acceptance test: *owns service and order-management strategy, sets escalation processes, accountable for service levels, fill rates, and CSAT; leads the CX team; partners with Supply Chain and Sales; owns order-to-cash; leads resolution of complex issues, deductions, and disputes; oversees SAP/ERP process compliance and data integrity; drives continuous improvement.* Every chapter of the homepage is mapped to at least one of these in `04-TARGET-JOB-TO-HOMEPAGE-MAP.md`. The page is, in effect, a working argument organized around the job description.

## Why it is product-first

A generic "CX dashboard" portfolio would prove nothing specific to Samyang. The differentiator is that FireFlow starts from Samyang's actual public portfolio — 45 normalized product families across Buldak, Samyang, Tangle, and MEP — and treats the **exact product** as the organizing spine. Selecting Buldak Carbonara in the hero changes the rail, the dossier, the comparison suggestions, and pre-populates any inquiry with that product's real, format-specific facts. This shows two things at once: genuine product understanding, and the core CX principle that you never make a customer re-enter what the system already knows.

## How Explore, Resolve, and Command connect

- **Explore** (Portfolio Pulse, Rankings Lab, Flavor Explorer, Comparison Lab, Dossier) — discover and understand products.
- **Resolve** (Two Paths, Resolution Simulator) — turn a product-specific consumer or vendor question into a verified, routed, governed case.
- **Command** (Command Center preview, Product Signals) — aggregate cases into a manager's operating picture and into portfolio improvement.

They connect through shared state: a product selected in Explore flows into Resolve; a case completed in Resolve appears in Command; a pattern in Command becomes an improvement signal. The visitor sees a single system, not three demos.

## What makes it different from a generic help desk

A help desk starts at the ticket. FireFlow starts at the **product** and at **evidence**. It separates verified facts from assumptions, requires approval for anything that commits money or a promise (credits, substitutions, delivery dates, deduction decisions), gives every case one accountable owner, makes the next customer update visible, and treats recurring cases as improvement work rather than repeated firefighting. It is a customer-experience *operating model*, expressed as an interface.

## Primary risks

Concentrated here, detailed in `KNOWN_LIMITATIONS.md` and the master plan: (1) rights to official product imagery; (2) misreading retail review counts as sales; (3) applying one variant's allergens/prep to a whole family; (4) over-claiming SAP experience; (5) scope creep across 18 deep chapters; (6) inventing vendor logistics data. Each has a standing mitigation.

## Recommended MVP

Ship the spine first: hero, chapter nav, selected-product rail, Portfolio Pulse, Rankings Lab, Comparison Lab, Product Dossier, the two inquiry paths, the resolution simulator, methodology, and FAQ — all working against the **six Tier-A Launch Anchors** (Buldak Carbonara, Original, 2X Spicy, Habanero Lime, Original Hot Sauce, Carbonara Hot Sauce) with full official data, plus all 45 families browseable with lighter, clearly-labeled data. Flavor Explorer, Command Center, Product Signals, Brand Universe, the mega navigation, and the full footer follow in later waves once routes and content are settled.
