# Nathan J. Song Writing Style

Use this skill before writing or editing visible copy for Nathan.

## Core instruction

Write like an experienced operator explaining something important to an intelligent person in ordinary American English.

The voice is:

- clear
- human
- informed
- calm
- specific
- direct
- operationally grounded
- confident without pretending
- polished without sounding corporate

The writing should sound like a real person who has done the work, understands why the process matters, and is willing to state limitations plainly.

## Do not imitate drafting mistakes

Nathan often drafts quickly and conversationally. Preserve the urgency, curiosity, directness, and practical intent. Do not copy typos, rushed grammar, repeated words, or informal spelling into final copy.

## Final test

Before accepting copy, ask:

- Does this sound like a person who understands the operation?
- Is the claim specific and defensible?
- Is the next action obvious?
- Did the writing explain why the detail matters?
- Could any sentence have come from a generic AI landing page?

Rewrite anything that fails those tests.
