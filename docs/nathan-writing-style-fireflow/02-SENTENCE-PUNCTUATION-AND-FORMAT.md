# Sentence, Punctuation, and Format Rules

## Sentence construction

Prefer strong, complete sentences with one main idea.

Good:

> The order is not ready to ship until the pricing exception has an owner.

Weak:

> By leveraging enhanced visibility across the order lifecycle, the organization can seamlessly drive alignment and improve outcomes.

Use a mix of short and medium sentences. Long sentences are allowed when the logic genuinely requires them, but the reader should never lose the subject.

## Punctuation rules

- Do not use em dashes as sentence separators.
- Avoid decorative arrows in visible copy, CTA text, labels, and navigation.
- Avoid unnecessary hyphens in normal sentences.
- Use commas, periods, colons, parentheses, or separate sentences instead.
- Do not use repeated exclamation points.
- Do not use ellipses to manufacture suspense.
- Use quotation marks only for actual labels, phrases, or quotations.

## Hyphen guidance

Hyphens are acceptable when grammatically required in established terms such as first-person, cross-functional, end-to-end, and order-to-cash. Do not force them into every compound phrase.

## Paragraphs

Keep paragraphs focused. Most visible website paragraphs should be two to four sentences.

## Headings

Headings should name the subject or outcome.

Preferred:

- Why the order is on hold
- What the customer already knows
- How the case becomes a prevention signal
- The operating decision behind this screen

Avoid:

- Unlocking the future
- Reimagining excellence
- A revolutionary experience
- Transforming possibilities

## Lists

Use lists when the reader needs to scan choices, steps, requirements, or evidence. Do not convert every paragraph into bullets.

## Capitalization

Use sentence case for most headings and controls. Avoid all caps except for compact labels where the visual system already uses them.

## Numbers and evidence

- Do not invent metrics.
- Do not add false precision.
- Label synthetic numbers clearly.
- When a number is important, explain what it represents.
