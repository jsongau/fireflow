# Approved Examples

## Entrance framing

> FireFlow is an independent customer operations concept created by Nathan J. Song for the Manager, Customer Experience opportunity at Samyang America. Explore it as a working operating model, or turn on Operator Notes to see the decisions behind each workflow.

## Independence statement

> FireFlow is not affiliated with Samyang America and does not represent access to its internal systems. Product information comes from public sources. Customers, orders, metrics, and outcomes are synthetic and labeled.

## Experience framing

> I have spent more than eight years working across consumer products, retail customer operations, billing, customer communication, and complex issue resolution. I built FireFlow to show how that experience translates into a structured order management and customer experience system.

## Product normalization note

> A customer sees one flavor. The operation may see several formats, packages, preparation methods, and data records. I normalized the portfolio so the interface stays simple without erasing the differences that matter.

## Inquiry routing note

> The customer should not have to decide which department owns the problem. The system should collect the right evidence, preserve the product context, and route the case without making the customer start again.

## Order exception note

> A hold is not a failure. An invisible hold is. The important questions are why the order stopped, who owns the next action, and what the customer has already been told.

## Billing note

> A billing dispute is rarely resolved by looking at the invoice alone. The operator needs the order, pricing condition, shipment evidence, customer agreement, and deduction reason in one view.

## Leadership note

> A standard should make good judgment easier to repeat. It should tell the team what evidence is required, when to escalate, who owns the response, and how the customer will be updated.

## Continuous improvement note

> Closing the case restores the customer. Reviewing the pattern protects the operation. I designed the signal loop to separate a single complaint from a repeated issue that deserves corrective action.

## SAP honesty note

> FireFlow is not an SAP implementation. This chapter demonstrates how I understand the operational sequence around order entry, pricing, delivery, billing, customer records, deductions, and resolution. It also shows how I would approach learning and supporting the company's SAP SD environment.

## Employer close

> I built FireFlow because a résumé can list responsibilities, but it cannot fully show how someone thinks through a complicated operation. This project demonstrates how I organize information, make ownership visible, protect the customer experience, and turn repeated issues into a better process.
