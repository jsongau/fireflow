# Operator Notes Voice

Operator Notes are Nathan's fourth-wall narration inside FireFlow. They should reveal judgment, not explain obvious interface actions.

## Purpose

Each note should answer at least one of these questions:

- What operational problem is this feature preventing?
- What information does a good operator need before acting?
- Who owns the next action?
- What does the customer need to know?
- Where does a process usually break?
- What did Nathan's experience teach him to notice?
- How does this connect to the target role?

## Tone

Sound like Nathan is standing beside the employer during a thoughtful product demonstration.

The note should feel personal, but not casual or self-congratulatory.

## Preferred structure

**Nathan's read**

1. Name the operational truth.
2. Explain why it matters.
3. Connect it to the design or workflow.
4. Add a brief first-person observation when useful.

## Strong examples

> A hold is not a failure. An invisible hold is. I designed this stage to show the reason, the owner, and the next customer update before the delay becomes a service problem.

> The customer should not need to understand the company's department chart. The system should preserve the context, route the request correctly, and show who is responsible for the next step.

> I tied the inquiry to the exact product and format because preparation, allergens, packaging, and routing can change between formats. The customer should never have to re-enter information the system already knows.

> An order can be entered correctly and still be operationally at risk. Pricing mismatches, credit holds, delivery changes, and billing disputes need visible ownership before they become repeated customer contacts.

> Closing the case is not the final step. A useful operation asks whether the same issue is likely to happen again and what evidence would support a permanent correction.

## Weak examples

> Click here to learn more about this section.

> This innovative feature provides a seamless user experience.

> I am highly skilled at customer experience and built this amazing system.

> This proves I am the perfect candidate.

## Frequency

Use Operator Notes selectively. Five or six meaningful notes across the main experience are stronger than constant commentary.

## Visual relationship

Operator Notes must look different from product UI. The visitor should immediately understand that the note is commentary from the builder, not a real status, alert, or company instruction.
