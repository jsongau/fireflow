# Fourth-Wall UX Rules

## Principle

Break the fourth wall at the threshold and at important teaching moments. Do not break it everywhere.

The employer should feel:

> Nathan is showing me how he thinks.

They should not feel:

> Nathan keeps interrupting his own product.

## Best structure

### Entry

Employer intro explains the project.

### Choice

Employer selects:

- Enter FireFlow
- Explore with Nathan

### Guided browsing

Operator Notes appear only when useful.

### Deep proof

Order-to-Cash Process Intelligence shows role alignment.

### Close

Final employer panel explains why FireFlow was built.

## Popups

Do not use random popups.

Allowed:

- One optional welcome choice
- One success modal after inquiry submission
- One final close panel
- Modals triggered by explicit user action

Not allowed:

- Auto popups on every section
- Notes that block browsing
- Repeated “hire me” interruptions
- Salesy lead capture behavior

## Side rail

Desktop side rail may show:

- current product
- current mode
- Operator Notes toggle
- quick jump to Order-to-Cash
- quick jump to Why I built this

It must not hide at common laptop widths.

Test widths:

- 1440px
- 1366px
- 1280px
- 1024px

## Mobile

Mobile should use:

- sticky bottom control or compact top toggle
- collapsed note cards
- clear headings
- no horizontal overflow

## Microcopy for toggle

Use:

- Operator Notes
- Nathan Mode
- Show Nathan’s thinking
- Hide Nathan’s thinking

Avoid:

- Break fourth wall
- Meta mode
- Candidate mode
- Hire me mode

## Inquiry submission modals

When a demo inquiry is submitted, the success modal should teach the workflow.

Example:

> Case opened in demo mode.
>
> In a real CX workflow, this is where the system would preserve the product, evidence, owner, severity, and next update. The goal is not only to answer the customer. The goal is to keep the organization aligned while the answer is being worked.

Buttons:

- View case timeline
- Continue exploring

## Final close panel

The final close should be clear, not desperate.

Use:

> I built FireFlow to show more than a résumé can show: how I learn a product system, structure customer and vendor questions, preserve operational context, and turn repeated friction into better training and process design.

Do not use:

> Please hire me.
