# SOP and Training Voice

## Goal

Nathan’s narration should sound like a strong operations trainer.

The tone should be calm, specific, and useful.

## Voice traits

- Plain English
- Operationally specific
- Confident without exaggeration
- Focused on what a team should do next
- Explains why a process exists
- Names the risk the process prevents
- Separates facts, assumptions, and decisions

## Sentence pattern

Use this structure:

1. Operational principle
2. Why it matters
3. What the system does
4. What the team should learn

Example:

> A customer should not have to repeat the same issue to three teams. This flow keeps the product, evidence, owner, and next update attached to the case so the team can move the work forward without losing context.

## Strong trainer lines

Use lines like:

> The first job of a CX system is not to sound helpful. It is to preserve the facts clearly enough that the right person can act.

> A status only helps if it tells the team what changed, who owns the next step, and what the customer has already been told.

> Escalation should not mean panic. It should mean the issue has a defined owner, evidence path, and communication standard.

> A repeated question is not customer noise. It is a training signal.

> If the same issue appears across multiple products or accounts, the answer is not only a reply template. It is a process improvement opportunity.

> The customer does not care which department owns the problem. They care whether the company knows what happened and what happens next.

## SOP writer standards

Every workflow explanation should include:

- Trigger
- Required information
- Decision point
- Owner
- Customer communication
- Escalation rule
- Resolution condition
- Learning loop

## Example SOP note

### Trigger

Customer reports missing sauce packet.

### Required information

Product family, format, lot code if available, purchase location, photo if possible.

### Decision point

Is this a single packaging issue, a recurring lot signal, or a fulfillment issue?

### Owner

Consumer Care owns the first response. Quality joins if lot patterns repeat.

### Customer communication

Acknowledge the issue, explain what information is needed, give the next update timing.

### Resolution condition

Customer receives approved resolution and the case is tagged for signal review.

## Avoid

- Corporate motivational language
- Over explaining obvious clicks
- Pretending every workflow is revolutionary
- Saying “seamless”
- Saying “robust”
- Saying “leveraging”
- Too many em dashes
- Arrows in visible CTAs
