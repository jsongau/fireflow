# SAP SD / Order-to-Cash Guardrails

## Purpose

Show SAP SD readiness without overstating direct SAP experience.

The goal is credibility.

## Preferred visible section title

> Order-to-Cash Process Intelligence

## Supporting label

> SAP SD aligned workflow study

## Approved explanation

> FireFlow is not an SAP implementation and does not claim access to Samyang’s internal systems. It is an independent front end operations concept that shows how Nathan understands the customer-facing workflow around order entry, pricing, delivery, billing, customer information, ownership, and resolution.

## What to demonstrate

### Order entry

Show that the case or order begins with a structured intake.

Include:

- customer or vendor identity
- product or SKU context
- issue or order type
- evidence needed
- owner
- next update

### Pricing

Show that pricing questions are not generic support questions.

Include:

- expected price
- invoiced or quoted price
- promotion or contract context
- who owns review
- what is communicated while review is pending

### Delivery

Show that delivery status should preserve what changed and who owns it.

Include:

- ship status
- delay reason
- carrier or logistics handoff
- customer or vendor update
- next commitment

### Billing

Show that billing inquiries need evidence, timestamps, and ownership.

Include:

- invoice number
- deduction or dispute reason
- supporting documentation
- finance owner
- resolution status

### Customer master data

Show why accurate records matter.

Include:

- customer name
- account type
- contact method
- shipping or billing context
- prior case history
- special handling notes

### Order management

Show the end-to-end timeline.

Include:

- intake
- validation
- routing
- owner assignment
- customer update
- resolution
- learning loop

## Approved claim language

Use:

- SAP SD aligned
- Order-to-Cash workflow study
- customer-facing workflow around SAP SD concepts
- front end operations concept
- designed to show how I would support and learn the workflow
- honest readiness to operate in an SAP-driven environment

Avoid:

- SAP expert
- SAP implementation
- SAP integration
- real SAP data
- production SAP tool
- eight years of SAP experience
- Samyang SAP replica
- end-to-end SAP build

## Employer-facing bridge

> My strongest experience is not pretending I have run Samyang’s SAP system. It is understanding the operational layer around a system: what the customer needs, what information must be accurate, what exception is blocking progress, who owns the next action, and how the update is communicated.

## Trainer-style explanation

> In a real SAP SD environment, the system of record matters. FireFlow does not replace that. It shows the communication and workflow layer that helps a CX team understand what is happening, what is blocked, and what the customer should hear next.

## Final standard

The SAP section should make the employer think:

> He is not exaggerating. He understands the process layer, knows what he still needs to learn, and can explain the workflow clearly.
