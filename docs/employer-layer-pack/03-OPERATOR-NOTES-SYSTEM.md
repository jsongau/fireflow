# Operator Notes System

## Purpose

Operator Notes let Nathan break the fourth wall without damaging the product illusion.

The employer should feel like Nathan is training them on the system, not interrupting them with portfolio captions.

## Modes

### Product Mode

Default product experience. Minimal Nathan voice.

### Explore with Nathan

Turns on contextual notes that explain the thinking behind the workflows.

### Operator Notes Toggle

Allows the employer to turn the notes on or off after entering.

## Note types

### 1. Nathan’s Read

A section-level explanation of the operational meaning.

Example:

> Nathan’s Read
>
> A customer should not have to understand our internal departments to get a clear answer. I designed this flow so the product, issue, evidence, owner, and next update stay attached from intake to resolution.

### 2. Role Connection

A concise link between a feature and the job requirement.

Example:

> Role connection: Shows how order intake, pricing questions, delivery status, billing inquiries, and customer records can stay connected in one service workflow.

### 3. Training Note

A short SOP-style explanation.

Example:

> Training note: Before routing a case, the system should capture what is known, what is missing, who owns the next action, and what the customer has already been told.

### 4. Honesty Note

A credibility guardrail.

Example:

> This is not an SAP implementation. It is a front end operations study that maps the customer-facing logic around SAP SD concepts.

## Placement rules

Use notes at high-leverage decision points:

- Entry intro
- Product selection
- Consumer inquiry
- Vendor inquiry
- Case simulator
- Command Center
- Product Signals
- Order-to-Cash chapter
- Final close

Do not add notes to every card, tab, or button.

## Density rules

Desktop:

- 1 note every major section
- Maximum 8 to 10 notes across the entire experience
- Notes may sit in a side rail if space allows

Mobile:

- Use collapsible cards
- Default to collapsed if the screen becomes crowded
- Notes must not cover primary controls

## Visual rules

Operator Notes must look different from product UI.

They should feel like:

- curator card
- field note
- SOP margin note
- trainer aside

They should not feel like:

- marketing popup
- chatbot bubble
- tooltip spam
- resume callout
- achievement sticker

## Sound and motion

Operator Notes may use subtle motion.

Rules:

- No automatic sound.
- Sound must be opt in.
- Respect reduced motion settings.
- No shaking, bouncing, or cartoon effects.
- Completion sounds should feel polished and restrained.

## Accessibility

- Toggle must be keyboard accessible.
- Notes must be readable by screen readers.
- Dismissed notes must not trap focus.
- Color cannot be the only indicator.
- Mobile notes must not overlap content.
