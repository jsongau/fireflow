# Employer Intro Linguistics

## Goal

The opening must create fast comprehension, not admiration hunting.

The employer should understand the project before they judge the design.

## What the intro must say

A strong intro answers:

1. This is FireFlow.
2. Nathan built it.
3. It is independent.
4. It was built for the role.
5. It demonstrates customer experience systems thinking.
6. The employer can browse product mode or guided mode.

## Recommended opening

> FireFlow is an independent customer experience operating model I built for the Manager, Customer Experience opportunity at Samyang America.
>
> It shows how I think through product knowledge, order flow, customer communication, escalation, billing friction, issue resolution, and continuous improvement.
>
> Explore it as a working product, or turn on Operator Notes to see the decisions behind each workflow.

## Button labels

Use:

- Enter FireFlow
- Explore with Nathan

Do not use:

- Learn more
- Start journey
- See my resume
- Discover innovation
- Click here

## Linguistic rules

### Prefer concrete nouns

Use:

- order flow
- customer record
- pricing exception
- billing inquiry
- delivery status
- escalation owner
- next update
- issue history

Avoid:

- solutions
- experiences
- transformation
- leverage
- robust platform
- seamless system

### Prefer operator verbs

Use:

- verify
- route
- assign
- preserve
- clarify
- resolve
- escalate
- document
- reconcile
- communicate

Avoid:

- revolutionize
- empower
- unlock
- enable at scale
- reimagine

### Keep sentences short at the entrance

The opening screen is not the case study. It is the door.

Target:

- 1 headline
- 2 to 3 short paragraphs
- 2 buttons
- 1 small honesty line

## Approved honesty line

> Independent concept. Not affiliated with Samyang America. Not an SAP implementation. Built to demonstrate customer experience, product operations, and Order-to-Cash thinking.

## Why this matters line

> For a consumer products company, customer experience is not only answering messages. It is knowing the product, understanding the order, protecting the customer relationship, and making sure Sales, Supply Chain, Finance, Quality, and Operations are working from the same truth.

## Bad intro pattern

Do not write:

> Nathan is a passionate, results-driven operations leader leveraging innovative systems to transform customer experience.

Why it fails:

- Generic
- Sounds AI written
- Does not show domain judgment
- Uses no real operational nouns

## Better pattern

Write:

> A customer case should not lose its product, owner, evidence, order status, or next update as it moves between teams.

Why it works:

- Specific
- Operational
- Trainer-like
- Immediately relevant to CX
